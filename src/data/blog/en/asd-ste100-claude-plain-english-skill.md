I was complaining a while back that Claude stopped talking like a human starting with 4.7. For a moment I thought my English just wasn't good enough, but then I hopped on Reddit and found native speakers complaining about it too.

But have you heard of ASD-STE100? It's an internationally used simplified-English standard, originally built for aircraft maintenance manuals, and the point is to let non-native speakers clearly understand the content of English technical documents. Simplified grammar, controlled vocabulary, so readers with a limited word count don't get confused.

Here's an example:

> The valve should be rotated and tested before continuing.

Gets rewritten into:

> Turn the valve. Do a test of the valve. Continue the procedure.

So I turned it into a skill, and every time Claude starts showing off its vocabulary again I push it to fall in line. Ever since then, I can finally understand its English.
